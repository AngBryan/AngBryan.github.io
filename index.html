Thoughts on Self exploration and more about myself

29 April 2022

From a very young age, I always believe in the notion of "self exploration". I remembered in primary school, I would try to mimick the style of the powerpoint slides that my teachers used in school when I got home or finding the source of where the cool game slideshow my teacher have taken it from. Even in my then CCA - Robotics, I was never really satisfied with just learning how to make the robot walk along the black line. I always craved for more and the only way to satisfy it is through searching online. At that time we were using Lego NXT and though I did not have the Lego pieces at home, I would just download the programming software and mess around with it to see what all the "blocks" do.

This idea of self exploration have carried over even in my secondary school years. This time I joined another CCA - Infocomm. Maybe I had too high expectations but I was once again dissatisfied with the instructors constantly covering the basics. This led to me discovering "Roblox" in my own free time (which is certainly a step up from scratch or other "drag or drop" programs). Google was my best friend throughout as I constantly had to read the wiki to learn about new functions or even just pasting the error codes and hope that StackOverflow has a solution. I got so invested to the point where I started exploring about exploitation and how we can easily effect changes and exploit vulnerabilities in the game through "client-side script executioners" (though client-side, there are ways to exploit a whole server!).

Now in JC I joined a cca close to tech but slightly different from what I have been doing - Channel News Nanyang (CNNY) /Filmmaking. Yes it is completely different from my past 2 ccas but the main reason was really due to the fact that I am tired of hoping to learn something new from someone when I can do the learning myself. This led to me learning the basics of Linux from Cisco Networking Academy as well as some basics of javascript from freecodecamp during the holidays.

Even this website was created through my own self exploration!
